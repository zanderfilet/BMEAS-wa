  • ✗


Hygiene Products



Soaps should be fragrance-free, since allergies are very common. Make sure any hand sanitizers do not contain alcohol. Toothpaste and toothbrushes also make practical and useful donation items.

  • ✗


Feminine Products



Tampons and pads are necessities, and yet something that many homeless individuals experiencing homelessness struggle to obtain every month. Consider different needs such as heavy, moderate, and light flow.

  • ✗


Water



Finding potable water can be difficult for homeless individuals. Bottled water is widely sold, affordable, and portable, making it a great donation item.

  • ✗


Bandages & First Aid Kits



Infection prevention and first aid care are essential, especially for homeless people. Give sealed, clean items like bandages, wipes, ointments, and nail care kits to protect those in need. Avoid packing alcohol-based products.

  • ✗


Healthy Snacks



Nonperishable and nutritious snacks such as nuts, dried fruit, applesauce, pretzels, and breakfast bars, are best. Foods with high protein are important. Fruit is good. Try to include some items that are easy to chew for individuals without access to dental care. Remember to pack food items separately from any scented sanitary products.

  • ✗


Clothes



These garments are daily essentials through all seasons, and yet we often take them for granted. Socks are especially crucial. They are affordable, easy to find, and overall great items to include.
